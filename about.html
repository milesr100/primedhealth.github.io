The Problem

Diabetes is a difficult disease to live with often upending the lifestyle of an individual who has been diagnosed. Frequent blood tests to determine whether blood glucose is in a safe range are often required, drastic changes in diet, increased doctor visit frequency, and many more lifestyle changes are strongly encouraged to better accommodate the disease for both Type one and Type 2 patients. (Buschur et al., 2017, pg. 373 ; Montejano et al., 2016, pg. 91; Diabetes Management, 2020). Additionally, current glucose monitoring solutions do not integrate into physician systems resulting in required assumptions about patient compliance.

Our Solution

The PrimedHealth App is a simple one designed with the age group predisposed to have Type 2 diabetes in mind; this happens to be middle aged people who are probably not as tech-savy as the younger generation. The app is built in a simple and straightforward design. It does not require input of any personal/secure information therefore there is no need to log in or out. This cuts down on set up time and makes this resource quick and easy to use in the moment. It also eliminates the security issue of possible breach as well as problems associated with passwords and forgetting them. Once downloaded and launched, the home page contains the logo, and two drop down menus from where users can select a state and city near them. Once a city is chosen, the address of the closest location for the Medicare Eligible Diabetes Prevention Program pops up. Also present at the bottom of the home page is a link that directs you to the cdc website for Diabetes prevention containing more information like resources, updates, healthcare professionals, employers and insurers and more..
